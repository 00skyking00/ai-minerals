E.6 spatial block-size empirical diagnostic

The v2 plan inherited a 20 km block size for spatial CV without empirical justification. E.6 runs a pre-flight diagnostic at 10 / 15 / 20 / 25 / 30 km and reports the trade-off: smaller blocks have more folds but more adjacency leakage; larger blocks lose statistical power but tighten the CV.

The 8-neighbour adjacency correlation is the “leakage risk” number: how correlated are positive counts in adjacent blocks. At 10 km blocks it’s 0.83 (adjacent blocks are nearly perfectly correlated; bad CV). At 30 km it drops to 0.46 (acceptable). v3’s planned 20 km default sits at 0.71.

E.5 leakage-risk audit

Finding: NO features flagged. And yet we know from v2 that pit-proximity leaks (RF AUC 0.995 vs LightGBM 0.804). What’s happening:

  • At 0.02% positive rate, the Pearson correlation of any feature with the binary label has an upper bound near 0.05 just from the prior. Even a perfectly-predictive feature would barely cross 0.10.
  • The v2 RF leakage was via PATTERN (cells near pit polygons get high score because cells AT pit polygons are positives). Pearson doesn’t see this — it’s not a linear correlation, it’s a local-neighborhood structure.

This is a real diagnostic limit. Simple correlation isn’t useful for small-positive leakage detection. The real diagnostics for that are:

  • E.4 SHAP recipe match: if hydraulic_pit_proximity_m_buffered dominates the top-5 Tertiary features it’s leakage even when |r| < 0.10.
  • Held-out generalization: if Lindgren PP73 secondary (de-duped 500m from any pit) scores 0 of 13 in the high tail it’s leakage.

v3.5 work: improve the leakage audit by adding nearest-neighbour positive density per cell as a leakage probe (the kind of structure Pearson misses).

E.4 SHAP recipe match

Implemented in the rationale script + the validation harness model card. Will produce real numbers when v3 Phase F.1 retrains both populations (the v2 estimator joblib has stale litho column names that the recipe-match helper doesn’t recognize cleanly; the v3 joblibs will be clean).

The Tertiary recipe expectations: top-5 must include ≥3 of {paleochannel_likelihood, distance_to_lode_m, hydraulic_pit_proximity_m_buffered, tertiary_terrace_likelihood, lithology_class_}. The Quaternary recipe expectations: top-5 must include ≥3 of {distance_downstream_from_lode_m, catchment_au_hawkes, spi_band, ksn, distance_to_lode_m, lithology_class_}.

If Tertiary’s top feature is the unbuffered hydraulic_pit_proximity_m we re-introduce the leakage shortcut and the recipe gate fails. The buffered variant in v3’s TERTIARY_FEATURE_COLUMNS is what we expect.
